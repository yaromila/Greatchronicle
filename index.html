Stephen Curry, Golden State Warriors

Daniel May
Aug 21 · 3 min read

In an effort to increase offense and make the NBA game more enjoyable for fans, in 1979, the NBA adopted the 3-point line. Over the past 35 years, fans have witnessed some truly legendary sharpshooters — Ray Allen, Reggie Miller, Steve Kerr, Glen Rice, Peja Stojakovic, Kyle Korver — just to name a few. Of all the great shooters to emerge over the past 35 years, one has clearly separated himself from the pack. After just six seasons in the NBA, Golden State Warriors long range specialist Steph Curry has already broken most 3-point records — and considering his age (27) Curry is on pace to shatter any 3-point record he doesn’t already own. Curry’s versatility makes him virtually impossible to guard. He can get his shot in a variety of ways — off the dribble, off a screen, in transition. Although defenses tirelessly scheme to contain Curry and limit his shot attempts, his variety of moves and split-second release allow him to make shots that most players wouldn’t dare attempting.


Record: Most 3-Pointers Made In A Single Season


On April 17th, 2013, against the Portland Trailblazers, Steph Curry broke Ray Allen’s record (269 3PM) for most 3-Pointers made in a season. Below are a comparison of their seasons. Ray Allen: 78 Games / 269 3PM / 653 3PA / .412% Steph Curry: 78 Games / 272 3PM / 600 3PA / .453% Curry broke Allen’s record on 53 LESS attempts.


Record: Most 3-Pointers In A Single Season Playoff


In the 2000 NBA playoffs, Hall of Famer Reggie Miller set an NBA playoff record with 58 3-Pointers on .395% shooting. It took Miller 22 games to set the record. In the 2015 NBA playoffs, Steph Curry broke Miller’s record in just 13 games! Curry finished the 2015 NBA playoffs with 98 total 3-Pointers in 21 games played — 40 more than Miller’s old record!
